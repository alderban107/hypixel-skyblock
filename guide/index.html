Hypixel SkyBlock

The Quintessential New Player Guide — 2025 / 2026

MC 1.21.10 (Fabric) All checkboxes save automatically Updated February 2026 Click any checkbox to track progress

Hypixel SkyBlock is an MMORPG built on top of Minecraft. Your goal is to level up skills, collect resources, unlock items, build an economy, and eventually tackle endgame content like Dungeons, Slayers, and Dragon fights. The game has years of content — this guide covers everything you need for a strong start without missing major power spikes.

Version Enforcement — 1.21.9+ Required (Feb 24, 2026) Starting February 24, 2026, Hypixel enforces a minimum of Minecraft 1.21.9 for SkyBlock — only the two most recent major versions are allowed. 1.8.9 is officially dead. This guide targets Minecraft 1.21.10 on Fabric, which is the version where all recommended mods have stable releases and the community modpack targets. Newer versions (1.21.11+) may work but not all mods are updated yet (e.g. Firmament currently has no 1.21.11 build). When in doubt, match the version the SkyBlock Enhanced modpack uses. The Hub is also getting a visual revamp with modern blocks alongside this change.

⚙️ Client Setup & Mods

Vanilla Minecraft is a terrible experience for SkyBlock. You need a mod launcher and several QoL mods before you even log in for the first time.

If You're Following Old Guides... Most SkyBlock YouTube tutorials and forum posts were made on Minecraft 1.8.9 with Forge. As of February 2026, 1.8.9 no longer works — Hypixel requires 1.21.9+. If you see guides referencing mods like NEU, Skytils, or SBA, those are 1.8.9-only and have no modern ports. Their features are now covered by Skyblocker, SkyHanni, and Firmament on Fabric. This guide targets the current version — ignore any setup instructions that mention Forge or 1.8.9.

Launcher (Pick One)

  • Prism Launcher — Best option. Free, open-source, handles Fabric/Forge/Quilt, multiple profiles. Drag-and-drop .mrpack modpack support. Install this, create a new Fabric 1.21.10 instance, and add mods below via the instance's "Edit Mods" button.
  • Modrinth App — Modern UI, good for searching and installing mods directly. Slightly less control than Prism.
  • Lunar Client — All-in-one, no mod management needed, built-in SkyBlock features. Best for total beginners. Press Right Shift in-game to access the SkyBlock config panel. Trade-off: less customizable than a full Fabric setup.

Essential Mods (Fabric 1.21.10)

Safety Warning Only download mods from their official Modrinth pages, GitHub repos, or official Discord servers. Fake mod downloads are common account-stealing malware (token loggers / RATs).

Skyblocker Must-Have

The primary SkyBlock mod for 1.21+. Dungeon puzzle solvers, Fairy Soul waypoints, Bazaar/AH price tooltips, experiment table solvers, backpack preview (hover + Shift), item wiki lookup (F4), Kuudra/mining overlays, and customizable HUDs. Your core mod — everything else builds on top of this.

modrinth.com → Skyblocker

Adds a searchable item sidebar to your inventory screen. On its own it only shows vanilla recipes — but Firmament feeds SkyBlock item data into it, giving you a browsable/searchable panel of every SkyBlock item. Not strictly required (Firmament has its own recipe viewer), but very convenient.

modrinth.com → REI

Complements Skyblocker with features it lacks: Garden crop milestone trackers, Diana burrow tracker (nearly mandatory for that event), Slayer helpers, Fishing overlays, Mining/Mineshaft detection, Rift features, and Chocolate Factory upgrade optimizer. 📋 First Day Checklist

Do these on a fresh profile immediately, in order. Every single item here is important and easily missed.

Follow the Tutorial Quests

SkyBlock has a built-in quest system. When you first spawn in the Hub, NPCs with green particles above their heads will guide you through the basics — talk to them. These quests teach core mechanics and give useful rewards.

The Quest Log Open your SkyBlock Menu → Quest Log to see all active and available quests. Many areas have quest chains that unlock new content, NPCs, or items. Don't skip these — they're designed to introduce systems you'd otherwise miss entirely.
  • Follow the Hub village tutorial NPCs Free Look for NPCs with green particle trails. They walk you through basic mechanics — banking, the Bazaar, crafting, and your island. Takes about 30 minutes and gives SkyBlock XP.
  • Complete the Lumberjack's quest in The Park Unlocks the path to Galatea (the Foraging island). Bring logs to NPCs for free armor rewards.
  • Do Rhys's quest in the Dwarven Mines Unlocks the Heart of the Mountain skill tree — essential for mining progression. Requires Mining 12.
  • Check the Quest Log regularly Quests give SkyBlock XP and sometimes unique items. New quests unlock as you access new areas and reach level milestones.

Priority Checklist

  • Claim your free Booster Cookie Free Go to the Community Center in Hub → talk to Elizabeth → claim one free cookie. Eat it immediately. Lasts 4 real days and gives "Keep Coins on Death" — huge when you're still learning. One-time per account.
  • Get the Rogue Sword from Jamie Free Jamie is near the water fountain in the Hub. This sword's ability gives a speed boost and is your movement tool until you get better gear. Don't leave Hub without it.
  • Register at the Bank Bank is in the Hub village. Deposit all coins immediately. If you die with coins on you, you lose 50% of your purse. The bank also pays interest. Get in the habit of banking everything.
  • Start the Chocolate Factory Free passive income Type /cf. Click the cookie 50 times to hire your first rabbit. It produces chocolate passively from now on forever. Costs nothing to start. Full guide in the Chocolate Factory section.
  • Open your SkyBlock menu and set up keybinds Press F (default) to open the SkyBlock Menu. Go to Settings → Keybinds. Set up your Accessory Bag, SkyBlock menu, and warp keys so they're accessible.
  • Buy starting tools from NPCs in Hub Farm Merchant: Rookie Hoe. Mine Merchant: Promising Pickaxe. Craft a Golden Axe (2 sticks + 3 gold from Mine Merchant) for foraging. These unlock your first collections immediately.
  • Talk to every NPC in the Hub village Many give free items, starter quests, or unlock features. Key NPCs: Adventurer (talisman shop), Blacksmith, Librarian, Farm Merchant, Mine Merchant. Don't skip the Lazy Miner near the Gold Mine entrance (free pickaxe with Auto Smelt).
  • Set up basic storage on your Private Island Type /is to go to your island. Place chests immediately. Even basic "Dump / Keep / Sell" organization helps. Your island is where your minions live — set it up early, it's much harder to reorganize later.
  • Start fishing to work toward 3 free Fairy Souls Your 2nd, 9th, and 24th treasure catches (not regular fish) are guaranteed Fairy Souls. Treasure is a rare loot category — base 5% chance per cast, increasing with Fishing level. This takes some patience but costs nothing. Start early so the catches accumulate naturally.
  • Configure Skyblocker: /skyblocker config Enable Fairy Soul Helper (under Helpers), Bazaar price tooltips, and backpack preview. Also open SkyHanni settings to enable Garden and Diana features. Press F4 while holding an item to open its wiki page.

Private Island Setup

Your Private Island is your base of operations. A little planning now prevents major headaches later when you have 20+ minions and need space for farms.

  • Set up organized storage Place chests in a central area. Label them (use signs or just remember): Keep, Sell to NPC, Crafting Materials, Bazaar Sell. This saves enormous time later when you're sorting drops.
  • Plan your minion layout Place minions in a grid with 2 blocks of space between each one — they need room to place/break blocks. Leave open space for future minions. You'll eventually have 20+ minion slots.
  • Build a crafting area Place your Enchanting Table, Brewing Stand, Anvil, and Experimentation Table (once unlocked) in one convenient spot. You'll visit these constantly.
  • Use island size upgrades — don't bridge outward Elizabeth sells island size upgrades for Bits. These expand your island properly. Building dirt bridges wastes time and looks terrible. Size upgrades also increase your minion cap.
  • Learn about Biome Sticks Some minions and farming crops require specific biomes (e.g., Cactus needs Desert, Mushroom needs Mushroom Island). Biome Sticks change your island's biome — buy from the Builder NPC or AH. Check requirements before placing specialized minions.

🛡️ Staying Safe — Scams & Security

SkyBlock has a serious scam problem. Losing months of progress to a preventable scam is the #1 reason new players quit. Learn these before you lose anything.

The Golden Rules Never add someone to your co-op that you don't fully trust. Never give items to a stranger who promises to "craft for you." Never download mods from anywhere except Modrinth, GitHub, or official Discord servers.

Common Scams

ScamHow It WorksHow to Avoid
Co-op Scam "I'll build farms on your island, just /coopadd me." Once added, they have full access to your island, chests, minions, and bank. There is no way to kick a player from a 2-person co-op without their cooperation. Never add strangers to your co-op. Ever. There is no legitimate reason for a stranger to need co-op access.
AH Item Swap Player shows a real item, agrees on a price, cancels trade, re-lists a worthless lookalike item. Vanilla textures make many items look identical. Use FurfSky Reborn texture pack to distinguish items visually. Always check item name, lore, and stats before buying.
Crafting Scam "I have all recipes unlocked, give me your materials and I'll craft it for you." They take your materials and leave. Unlock recipes yourself. Use /ct to craft. If you truly need someone else to craft, use a trusted guild member — never a random.
Fake Mods / RATs Discord DMs or chat links offering "free SkyBlock mods" that are actually token loggers or Remote Access Trojans. They steal your Minecraft session and can access your real computer. Only download from Modrinth, GitHub, or official mod Discord servers. Never click links in-game or in DMs from strangers.
"Quitting" Auction "I'm quitting SkyBlock, highest bid in chat gets all my stuff!" Always a lie designed to extract coins. Ignore completely. Real quitters sell on AH or give to friends.
Dungeon Carry Scam Payment disagreements — one party pays upfront and the "carry" leaves, or the carry completes the run and the buyer doesn't pay. Use reputable carry services through established guilds/Discord servers. Never pay random players in chat.
Casino / Gambling Islands Player-run casinos on private islands. Always rigged, and also against Hypixel rules — you can be banned for participating. Never gamble. Report casino advertisers.

SkyBlock has two separate marketplaces. Understanding the difference saves you money from day one.

Always use Buy Orders on the Bazaar. Instant Buy costs 5-15% more than a Buy Order for the same item. Place a Buy Order at slightly above the current order price and it fills in seconds — same speed, much cheaper. The same applies in reverse: use Sell Offers instead of Instant Sell to get more coins.
AH Flipping The Auction House is where experienced players make money by buying underpriced BIN items and reselling. As a beginner, focus on using BIN to buy gear — avoid bidding wars, you'll usually overpay. Use /ah [player] to inspect what someone is wearing if you want to buy the same item.

⚔️ Stats & How Damage Works

SkyBlock has its own stat system layered on top of Minecraft. Every piece of gear, every reforge, every potion, and every skill level contributes stats that determine how hard you hit and how much damage you can take. Understanding this system is the difference between efficient gearing and wasting millions on the wrong upgrades.

Core Stats Reference

The Damage Formula

Melee damage in SkyBlock follows this formula:

Damage = (5 + Weapon Damage) × (1 + Strength / 100) × (1 + Crit Damage / 100)
This only applies on critical hits. Non-crits skip the Crit Damage multiplier entirely, which is why they feel so weak.

The key insight: Strength and Crit Damage are multiplicative with each other. Having 200 Strength and 200 Crit Damage deals more damage than having 100 Strength and 300 Crit Damage, even though the total stats are the same. Keep them roughly balanced for maximum output.

Why 100% Crit Chance Comes First

A non-critical hit removes the (1 + CritDamage/100) multiplier entirely. With typical mid-game stats of 200% Crit Damage, that means non-crits deal one-third of a crit's damage. If your Crit Chance is only 60%, you're losing 40% of your hits to non-crits — a massive DPS loss no amount of Strength or Crit Damage can compensate for.

  • Priority 1: Get Crit Chance to 100% (reforges, accessories, armor, God Potion)
  • Priority 2: Balance Strength and Crit Damage roughly equally
  • Priority 3: Increase both Strength and Crit Damage together

How Defense Works

Defense reduces incoming damage using the formula: Damage Taken = Raw Damage × (100 / (100 + Defense))

This means Defense has diminishing returns on paper, but each point always gives the same amount of Effective HP:

Effective HP = Health × (1 + Defense / 100). Both Health and Defense contribute equally to survivability — don't stack one and ignore the other. A balanced build survives better than an all-Defense or all-Health build.

📕 Bestiary

🚀 Early Game Progression

Work through these phases in order. Don't rush — each phase sets up the next one. The goal of early game is to unlock systems, not to maximize coins.

Phase 1 — Movement & Survival

  • Get Rogue Sword (from Jamie in Hub, free) Speed boost ability makes early exploration bearable.
  • Farm wheat in Hub fields until Wheat Collection III Unlocks Farm Suit armor recipe — your first proper armor set. Sell extra wheat and seeds to the Farm Merchant.
  • Craft and equip Farm Suit armor Requires Wheat III collection. Open your crafting table with /ct.
  • Get an Undead Sword from the Blacksmith or early combat Deals bonus damage to undead mobs. Good cheap weapon for early grinding.
  • Place your first Minion on your island Craft a Cobblestone Minion using the SkyBlock crafting table (wooden pickaxe in center surrounded by cobblestone). Place it on your island (/is). Minions generate resources 24/7 even while you're offline — start this immediately. See the Minions section for expansion priority.

Phase 2 — Unlock the Accessory Bag

This is your single most important early goal. The Accessory Bag holds your talismans, which give Magical Power — the biggest stat multiplier in the game.

  • Do the Lazy Miner quest at Gold Mine entrance Gives a pickaxe with Auto Smelt — ore goes straight to ingots, no furnace needed. Free quality of life.
  • Reach Mining Level 5 (mine coal, iron, gold in the Gold Mine) Required to enter Deep Caverns, which has Redstone.
  • Enter Deep Caverns and mine Redstone in the Pigmen's Den Pass through Gunpowder Mines → Lapis Quarry → Pigmen's Den to find Redstone. This is the slow part — push through it.
  • Craft or buy Lapis Armor while in the Lapis Quarry You pass right through the Lapis Quarry on the way to Redstone. Mine Lapis Lazuli and craft full Lapis Armor, or buy from AH for ~10k total. Each piece gives +50% bonus XP from ores (Magnetic bonus — this is vanilla XP, not SkyBlock Mining skill XP) — wear the full set when mining for +200% total. Also has decent defensive stats for early combat. Keep Farm Suit for gathering.
  • Reach Redstone Collection II — unlock the Accessory Bag Open your SkyBlock menu → Your Bags → Accessory Bag. Every talisman now goes here instead of taking inventory slots.
  • Go to Community Center and talk to Elizabeth at SkyBlock Level 3 Start Account Upgrades in this priority: Accessory Bag slots, Ender Chest pages, Minion slots. These are time-gated — they take real-life hours to complete, so start them on day 1. Right-click upgrades to start the free slow-speed version. These apply to all profiles permanently.

Phase 3 — Fill the Bag, Unlock the Bazaar & Maxwell

You have an Accessory Bag but it's empty. Time to fill it, unlock the Bazaar (SkyBlock Level 7), and visit the most important NPC new players miss — Maxwell.

  • Buy cheap talismans from the Adventurer NPC in Hub Zombie Talisman (500), Skeleton Talisman (500), Village Affinity (2,500), Mine Affinity (2,500), Scavenger (10,000), Intimidation (10,000). Put them ALL in your Accessory Bag — each one adds Magical Power.
  • Craft collection-based talismans as you unlock them Vaccine Talisman (Potato III), Speed Talisman (Sugarcane II), Farming Talisman (Wheat IV), Talisman of Coins (Emerald II), Healing Talisman (Lily Pad III), Spider Talisman (Spider Eye II). See the full list in the Accessories section. Every talisman = more MP = more damage.
  • Place 1–3 Redstone Minions on your island Redstone Collection directly expands your Accessory Bag slots. Minions passively grind the collection while you do other things. Put a Compactor in each one. This is high priority.
  • Level Mining, Farming, and Foraging to 5 each This gets you to SkyBlock Level 7, which unlocks the Bazaar — one of the biggest early progression gates. Mine in Gold Mine/Deep Caverns, farm wheat in Hub, chop trees in The Park.
  • Unlock and use the Bazaar (SkyBlock Level 7) Type /bz to open it. You can now buy and sell materials to other players. NPC prices are almost always terrible — always check Bazaar first. Use Buy Orders (cheaper) instead of Instant Buy when you can wait.
  • Get your first pet Buy a Bee pet from AH (~10k coins) or craft a Rock pet (Cobblestone Collection III). Equip it from your SkyBlock Menu → Pets. Pets give passive stat bonuses and earn Taming XP while you play. Always have one equipped — there's no reason not to.
  • Visit Maxwell at the Thaumaturgist in Hub From the Hub spawn, look for the sandstone building behind the quartz buildings. Maxwell lets you set a Power (stat buff that scales with your Magical Power) and Stat Tuning points. Pick Fortuitous as your first Power — it gives Crit Chance. Then put all your Tuning points into Crit Chance until you hit 100%. This is one of the biggest free damage boosts in the game and most new players never touch it.
  • Reach 100% Crit Chance Base is 30%. Combat levels give +0.5% each. Maxwell's Fortuitous power + Tuning + weapon reforge (Sharp) gets you the rest. Without 100% CC, your damage is wildly inconsistent — some hits deal full damage, others don't crit at all. This is THE early game priority.
Early Money Tip — NPC Flipping Once you have Bazaar access, buy items cheaply from NPCs and sell them on the Bazaar. Some profitable flips: Packed Ice from Sherry (9 coins → higher on Bazaar), Sand, Flint, Iron Ingots, String, Gunpowder. It's not exciting but it funds your first AH purchases (AOTE, Piggy Bank, etc.).

Phase 4 — Movement & The Park

  • Craft a Grappling Hook (String Collection IV) Makes traversal much faster. Kill spiders in Spider's Den for string, or buy from Bazaar. Game-changing movement tool before you get AOTE.
  • Get Aspect of the End (AOTE) (Ender Pearl Collection VIII or buy from AH) Teleports you forward on right-click. Best early-game weapon AND movement tool. Worth buying from AH if you can afford it — around 200-400k coins. Use NPC flipping or Bazaar profits to fund this.
  • Consider getting a Zombie Sword Heals you on ability use. Great for surviving while you're still learning the game. Cheap on AH.
  • Enchant your weapons and armor Build an Enchanting Table on your island (4 Obsidian + 2 Diamonds + 1 Book) and surround it with bookshelves (each = 1 Bookshelf Power). 20 power is enough for most enchants. Or craft just 2 Enchanted Bookshelves (15 power each — requires Sugar Cane Collection VI) and skip the pile entirely. Apply Sharpness V and Knockback II to your AOTE, Protection V and Growth V to your armor. Even basic enchants are a massive power spike. See the Enchanting section.
  • Do The Park quest chain for free armor pieces The Park is the foraging island (Foraging Level 1 to access). Climb through wood zones — each NPC gives a "bring logs" quest with free gear rewards: Charlie's Trousers (64 Birch Logs), Kelly's T-Shirt (128 Spruce Logs), Mole Hat (512 Jungle Logs), Melody's Shoes (512 Acacia Logs). Not best-in-slot, but they're free and help early.
  • Get the Campfire Badge from the Trial of Fire In The Park's Dark Thicket, talk to Ryan. Stand in the campfire for 10 seconds to earn the Campfire Badge — a free accessory that upgrades as you beat harder trials. Come back when your HP is higher to upgrade it further.
  • Start collecting Fairy Souls Enable Fairy Soul waypoints in Skyblocker (/skyblocker config → Helpers → Fairy Soul Helper). There are 79 souls in Hub alone — collect them as you pass through areas. Turn in every 5 to Tia the Fairy (Wilderness) for SkyBlock XP and Backpack slots. See the Fairy Souls section.

Phase 5 — The Garden & Farming

  • Unlock The Garden (SkyBlock Level 5) Type /warp garden. You start with 1 plot. The Garden is your personal farming island with its own leveling system, crop milestones, and visitor NPC mechanic.
  • Unlock your first 3 plots and plant diverse crops Plots unlock as your Garden Level rises. Plant Wheat, Carrot, and Potato to start their milestones — each crop has separate Fortune stats and milestone rewards. Diversity matters more than volume early on.
  • Accept Garden Visitors NPCs appear on your Garden and request crops. Completing their orders gives Garden XP (unlocks more plots), Copper (for upgrades), and rare rewards. Never skip visitors — they're your main source of Garden XP early.
  • Farm Pumpkin for Rancher's Boots, then Mushroom for XP Pumpkin milestone gives Rancher's Boots (lets you set your speed to any value — essential for efficient farming). Then switch to Mushroom for the best Farming XP in the game. See the full Garden section for detailed progression.

Phase 6 — Combat Progression

  • Grind Spider's Den for Combat XP Spiders and Cave Spiders give solid Combat XP. Good zone to practice combat and build toward Spider Slayer later. Graveyard (Hub) is also great for early Zombie grinding.
  • Grind Crypt Ghouls under the Hub Graveyard Crypt Ghouls are easy one-shots with decent gear and give strong Combat XP. The Graveyard also has zombies that drop Poisonous Potatoes for Vaccine Talisman (Potato Collection III) and Nether Wart growing nearby — pick some for Potion Affinity Talisman (Nether Wart Collection III).
  • Work toward Strong Dragon Armor Buy from the AH (~3–5M for a full set) or craft from Dragon Fragments. Requires Combat 16 to wear. Great all-purpose mid-game armor. See the End section for the full progression path.
  • Reach The End and start the Ender Armor progression At Combat 12, you arrive at The End. Buy the Void Sword from the Lone Adventurer, then farm Endermen for Ender Armor (stats doubled on The End) and the Ender Equipment set. This is your on-ramp — you won't be ready for Zealots until you have Dragon Armor + AOTD + enchants. See the End section for details.
  • Reach Combat Level 15 — unlocks the Catacombs Entrance This is your major mid-game milestone. Don't rush it — have decent gear before entering Catacombs. See the Dungeons section for floor-by-floor requirements.
  • Start Zombie Slayer (T1–T2) from Maddox in the Hub Once you have Dragon Armor and AOTE/AOTD, Revenant Horror T1–T2 is easy. This unlocks slayer-exclusive accessories (free Magical Power), the Revenant equipment line, and starts progression toward higher tiers. Slayers run parallel to Dungeons — do both. See the Slayer Bosses section.
Dragon Fight Tips You don't need all 8 Summoning Eyes yourself — other players in The End also contribute eyes. Placing more eyes gives you a larger share of the loot. The dragon type is random: Superior Dragon is the rarest and most valuable. Use an AOTD or strong weapon and aim for high damage placement to maximize your loot share.

💎 Accessories & Magical Power

Magical Power (MP) is one of the most impactful stats in the game. You get it from accessories based on their rarity. More MP = significantly more damage, defense, and health. This system has no ceiling — you're always improving it.

Critical Rule Accessories ONLY give Magical Power when they are inside your Accessory Bag, NOT in your inventory. Accessories sitting in your inventory are completely useless. Always move them to the bag.
Only the Highest Tier Counts Accessories come in upgrade chains: Talisman → Ring → Artifact → Relic. If you have a Speed Talisman and a Speed Ring, only the Ring's MP counts. Always upgrade to the highest tier you can afford.

Maxwell Powers & Stat Tuning

Visit Maxwell at the Thaumaturgist in Hub (sandstone building near the quartz area). Maxwell lets you assign a Power to your Accessory Bag — a stat buff that scales with your total Magical Power. He also gives Tuning Points (1 per 10 MP) that you allocate to specific stats.

  • Set your Power to Fortuitous Fortuitous gives the most Crit Chance of any Power — best early choice. You can change Powers anytime for free. Later (with 300+ MP), consider switching to Silky or Bloody for damage optimization.
  • Put all Tuning Points into Crit Chance until you hit 100% Open Maxwell's menu → Stats Tuning (bottom-right). Pump Crit Chance until your stat screen shows 100%. After that, shift points into Crit Damage or Strength. Check your stats with /sbmenu → Your Profile.
  • Verify you're at 100% Crit Chance Base: 30%. Combat levels: +0.5% each. Fortuitous power: scales with MP. Tuning: variable. Weapon reforge (Sharp): +10%. Without 100% CC, some hits skip the crit damage multiplier entirely — your damage will feel random and weak.

Reforging

Visit the Blacksmith and reforge your accessories. Itchy is a good default reforge for early game — it gives +Critical Damage which benefits your Magical Power scaling. Later you'll want to look into optimal reforge setups as your MP grows.

For weapons, Sharp gives Crit Chance (good if you're not at 100% yet), while Spicy gives more damage once you are. For armor, Fierce is the best regular reforge, or Pure if you still need more Crit Chance.

The Wardrobe

SkyBlock requires frequent armor swapping — farming set, combat set, mining set, speed set. The Wardrobe lets you save and swap full armor sets instantly instead of manually equipping 4 pieces every time.

  • Access: Use /wd or open the SkyBlock Menu → click the armor icon
  • Unlocked at SkyBlock Level 5, starting with 2 wardrobe slots
  • More slots unlock as your SkyBlock Level increases — up to 18 slots total at high levels

✨ Enchanting, Potions & Buffs

Three of the biggest power spikes in SkyBlock come from enchanting your gear, using the Experimentation Table daily, and the God Potion. All three are frequently overlooked by new players.

Essential Enchantments

Enchant your gear as early as possible. Build an enchanting table on your island with at least 20 Bookshelf Power (20 regular bookshelves, or just 2 Enchanted Bookshelves) for access to all enchantments. Here's what to put on your stuff:

Sword Enchantments

Must-Have

Sharpness V (or Smite V if you mostly fight undead — better for early Zombie Slayer), Critical V (or VI if cheap), Giant Killer V, Execute V, First Strike IV (for one-shot mobs) or Triple-Strike V (for tanky mobs), Ender Slayer V (essential for The End), Lethality V, Cubism V.

Good to Have

Life Steal III (heal on hit — great for early survival), Scavenger III (extra coins from mobs), Experience III, Looting III, Vampirism V.

Avoid

Knockback — never put this on anything. It pushes mobs away, wasting your time. Fire Aspect — the fire ticks can break First Strike on your next target.

Armor Enchantments

Protection V and Growth V on every piece. Protection = defense, Growth = HP. These are the two most important armor enchants.
Thorns III — warning: this can interfere with First Strike on your sword (the mob takes Thorns damage first, cancelling the bonus). Skip this if you rely on First Strike.

Pickaxe / Tool Enchantments

Efficiency V, Fortune III, Experience III. Optionally Smelting Touch (auto-smelts ores) OR Silk Touch (can't have both). Keep two pickaxes — one for each.
Compact (auto-compacts mined items) is excellent if you can get it.

  • Build an enchanting table on your island with 20 Bookshelf Power You need 20 Bookshelf Power for all enchantments — that's 20 regular bookshelves (each = 1 power) or just 2 Enchanted Bookshelves (each = 15 power, requires Sugar Cane Collection VI).   • Put Protection V + Growth V on every armor piece
  • Apply Hot Potato Books to weapons and armor you'll keep Each HPB adds +2 Strength and +2 Defense to weapons, +4 HP and +2 Defense to armor (up to 10 per item). Buy from Bazaar — cheap and massive stats gain.

The God Potion

The single biggest combat power spike in SkyBlock. One potion applies 33 potion effects at max level simultaneously, including +100 Health, +99 Strength, +100 Intelligence, +88 Magic Find, Critical IV, Regeneration IX, and more.

🎒 Sacks & Inventory Management

Sacks are special bags that auto-collect specific resource types as you pick them up. Without sacks, your inventory fills up in minutes and you're constantly going back to your island to dump items. They are essential for all content.

How they work: When you have a sack in your inventory, matching items you pick up go directly into the sack's storage (not your inventory). Right-click a sack to open it and collect items, or use /gfs [item] to retrieve items from any sack. You can also sell sack contents directly through the Bazaar without removing them.

  • Craft a Mining Sack Stores all ores and mining drops. Essential for Dwarven Mines, Crystal Hollows, and any mining content.
  • Craft a Combat Sack Stores mob drops (bones, rotten flesh, string, etc.). Essential for grinding and Slayer quests.
  • Craft an Agronomy Sack Stores farming drops. Essential once you start The Garden.
  • Unlock the Sack of Sacks (Tropical Fish Collection IV) Stores sacks in a meta-sack in your SkyBlock Menu (bottom right). Starts with 3 slots, expands through Tropical Fish collection and Account Upgrades. Without this, each sack takes an inventory slot.
  • Get a Personal Compactor 4000 (Redstone Collection IX) Automatically compresses raw materials into enchanted versions as you pick them up. Pairs with sacks — raw items get compacted, then the enchanted forms go into your enchanted sacks. Massive inventory efficiency.
Priority Order Get whichever sack matches what you're doing first. Mining Sack and Combat Sack are the most universally useful. Then unlock the Sack of Sacks so they don't eat inventory slots.

Dungeons are procedurally generated mazes with puzzles, traps, mini-bosses, and a final boss fight. Some of the strongest gear in the game is locked behind dungeons. The Catacombs Entrance unlocks at Combat Level 15.

Don't Rush Dungeons Don't enter the Catacombs the second you hit Combat 15. Aim for at least 300 Magical Power and decent gear first. Non-dungeonized gear is nearly useless past Floor 2 — read the Essence & Stars section below before entering.

Essence, Stars & Dungeonizing

Dungeon gear has its own power system. Understanding this before entering Dungeons will save you enormous frustration.

  • Dungeonizing — Items must be converted into "Dungeon Items" to scale with your Catacombs level. Items that drop inside Dungeons are pre-dungeonized. Items bought from AH (like Dragon Armor) must be manually dungeonized at Malik (Dungeon Hub NPC) using Essence. Without this, your AH gear does almost nothing in Dungeons.
  • Star Upgrades — Each star adds +10% stats in Dungeons (up to 5 stars = +50%). Combined with Catacombs level scaling, a 5-starred item at high Cata level gets hundreds of percent bonus stats. Stars are applied at Malik using Essence.
  • Master Stars — Additional stars from Master Mode floors (M1, M3, M5, M6, M7), each adding +5% in Master Mode. Endgame only.
  • Item Quality — Dungeon drops have a hidden quality stat (1–50). A quality-50 item has up to 49% more base stats than quality-1. When buying dungeon drops from AH, check quality — the cheapest listing isn't always the best value.
Essence Shops — Free Permanent Perks There are multiple Essence Shops (Undead, Wither, Dragon, Spider, Crimson, Ice, Gold, Diamond, and more), each selling permanent stat perks for their Essence type. Early priority at Malik's Undead Essence shop: Boss Luck (better dungeon loot) and Help of the Fairies (free revive stones from Fairy Souls). Check all shops periodically as you accumulate Essence.

Picking a Class

There are 5 classes: Healer, Mage, Berserk, Archer, Tank. Duplicate classes in a party reduce each copy's effectiveness — spread out classes for full power. Tips:

  • Berserk — Best choice for most beginners. Damage bonus, straightforward playstyle.
  • Mage — High damage ceiling, great endgame. Slightly more complex.
  • Archer — Weak early, strong later (Juju Shortbow at Floor V+).
  • Healer — Support role. Good in parties, awkward solo.
  • Tank — Very tanky, low damage. For experienced players who want a different challenge.

Floor Progression Checklist

  • Reach Combat Level 15 and enter the Catacombs Entrance Talk to Mort in the Dungeon Hub (accessible from Hub) or use Abiphone. You need to party up (2-5 players) unless you get Saul's Recommendation from defeating The Professor in Floor III.
  • Run the Entrance, then progress through Floors I–III Learn puzzle mechanics and room types on the Entrance. Move to Floor I at Cata 1, Floor II at Cata 3, Floor III at Cata 5. Don't grind Entrance longer than needed — each floor gives more XP.

  • Farm Floor 4 (Cata 9) — first real gear upgrades Get a Frozen Scythe (Mage) or equivalent for your class. Floor 5 unlocks at Catacombs Level 14.
  • Farm Floor 5 (Cata 14) — key gear drops Shadow Assassin Armor drops here — major upgrade for most classes.

Dungeon Score System Your score is based on: Skill (deaths, puzzle fails), Exploration (secrets found, room completion %), Speed, and Bonus factors. Higher score = better reward chests at the end. Aim for S+ for maximum rewards. Secrets are hidden rooms/chests — use the Skyblocker dungeon map to find them.

The Park and Galatea are the home of Foraging after the June 2025 rework. The Park is your introduction — Galatea is where the real progression happens. This section covers both islands end-to-end, from your first axe swing to endgame money-making.

The Park — Getting Started

The Park is accessible from the Hub portal (or /warp park). It's the starting zone for Foraging — simple trees, introductory NPCs, and a quest chain that teaches you the reworked mechanics.

🐉 The End

The End unlocks at Combat Level 12 and is your first serious combat zone. You won't be one-shotting everything on arrival — there's a real gear progression here. You'll start by farming Endermen for Ender Armor, work your way up to Dragon Armor, and eventually farm Zealots for Summoning Eyes. This island is where early game becomes mid game.

Arriving & Early Progression

  • Reach Combat Level 12 and travel to The End Access via the portal in Spider's Den, a private island Portal to The End, or /warp end with a Travel Scroll. Deposit your coins before going — deaths drop coins from your purse.
  • Talk to the Lone Adventurer and buy the Void Sword (200k) He starts a quest line that walks you through the island. The Void Sword gains +20 Strength for every Ender Armor piece you're wearing (4 armor + 4 equipment pieces = 8 max, +160 Strength). This is your weapon for The End until you can afford an AOTD.
  • Farm top-level Endermen for Ender Armor Ender Armor pieces drop at a 1% rate from Endermen. The set bonus doubles all armor stats while on The End, making it extremely strong for this island specifically. Enchant and reforge each piece as you get it — after 2 pieces + the Void Sword, you should be one-shotting the upper Endermen.
  • Collect the Ender Equipment set Ender Necklace (0.5% from Endermen), Ender Cloak (1% from Seers in Dragon's Nest), Ender Belt (1% from Obsidian Defenders in Dragon's Nest), Ender Gauntlet (from mining Ender Nodes). Each piece counts toward the Void Sword's Strength bonus and fills your Equipment slots.
  • Mine Ender Nodes whenever you see them Shiny blocks scattered around the island. They drop loot, Grand Experience Bottles (useful for enchanting), and occasionally the Ender Gauntlet. Mining End Stone also has a 5% chance to spawn Endermites.
  • Complete the End Race for the Pig's Foot accessory Talk to Guber (left of the entrance). Beat the race in under 48 seconds to earn the Pig's Foot — a Rare accessory that gives +1 Speed. Use AOTE teleport to cut corners. Beating it in 42 seconds earns an achievement.
  • Get a Rare Enderman Pet Drops from Endermen in The End or buy from AH (~100–200k). Solid mid-game combat pet for this zone.
Don't Skip Ender Armor New players often try to jump straight to Dragon Armor or Zealots — this doesn't work. Ender Armor with the Void Sword is your actual progression path on The End. The 2x stat multiplier on The End makes it competitive with much more expensive sets while you're here.

Zealot Farming (Mid-Game)

Zealots are Level 55 mobs with 13,000 HP that spawn in the Dragon's Nest area. Killing one has a 1/420 chance to spawn a Special Zealot, which is guaranteed to drop a Summoning Eye (~800k–1.5M on Bazaar). This is a solid mid-game money method, but it requires real gear investment first.

Gear Check You need 13,000+ damage in a single hit to one-shot Zealots — they heal to full if you don't kill them instantly. An AOTE alone won't get you there. You typically need: Strong Dragon Armor (or Unstable) + AOTD (or a well-enchanted Void Sword with full Ender set) + Sharpness V, Ender Slayer V, Giant Killer V, First Strike IV + Hot Potato Books + a filled Accessory Bag with combat reforges + a God Potion. If you can't one-shot them, you're not ready yet — keep leveling Combat and building your gear.
  • Reach Combat 16+ and get Strong Dragon Armor + AOTD Strong Dragon Armor (Combat 16, ~3–5M on AH) buffs your AOTE with +75 Damage and +2 teleport range. The AOTD (~5–8M on AH) is a major damage upgrade. Together with enchants and accessories, this is the typical Zealot farming loadout.
  • Test your damage — can you one-shot Zealots? Hit a Zealot. If its HP bar doesn't disappear instantly, you need more damage. Check: weapon enchants (Ender Slayer V is huge here), Hot Potato Books, Accessory Bag Magical Power with combat-oriented reforges (Hurtful on common, Shaded/Strong on higher), God Potion, and 100% Crit Chance.
  • Listen for the Special Zealot spawn sound When you trigger a Special Zealot, you'll hear a Wither spawn sound and get a chat notification. Kill it fast — other players will try to snipe it. The game has bad luck protection: if you go too long without a drop, your chances double.
  • Try the Zealot Bruiser Hideout for better odds Portals in the Dragon's Nest lead to the Hideout, where Zealot Bruisers have a 1/380 Special Zealot spawn rate (vs 1/420). They're tougher but give more Combat XP and better drop odds.

Dragon Fights

Place 8 Summoning Eyes in the End Altar at the Dragon's Nest to spawn an Ender Dragon. You don't need all 8 yourself — other players in the lobby contribute eyes too. Placing more eyes gives you a bigger share of the loot. The dragon type is random.

Dragon Fight Tip When a dragon spawns, the Dragon's Nest gets sealed with glass. Loot is based on damage placement — deal as much damage as possible with your best weapon. Placing eyes also boosts your loot weight, so even contributing 1–2 eyes is worthwhile.

How the Rift Works

The Rift is fundamentally different from normal SkyBlock. Understanding these mechanics before entering saves a lot of confusion:

  • Rift Time replaces Health. You start with 8 minutes. It counts down constantly once you leave the Wizard Tower. Mobs drain extra time instead of dealing damage. When it hits 0, you're kicked back to the Hub.
  • Rift Damage replaces all combat stats. You start with 20 Rift Damage. Your normal Strength, Crit Chance, Crit Damage — none of it exists here.
  • Motes replace Coins. Earned from killing mobs, completing quests, and collecting pink orbs (+25 Motes and +2 Rift Time per orb).
  • Your gear doesn't work. Armor, weapons, accessories, potions — nothing from normal SkyBlock carries over. You use Rift-specific gear only.
  • No fall damage in the Rift.
  • Time is paused in the Wizard Tower (except bottom floor), Rift Gallery, and Mirrorverse. Half-speed near the Colosseum.
Don't Panic About the Timer 8 minutes feels impossibly short, but it improves fast. Enigma Souls (+20 each, 52 total = +1,040 seconds), the Montezuma pet (+15 per fragment), and Timecharms all add Rift Time. After your first few visits collecting souls, you'll have plenty of time. Collect orbs constantly — they restore +2 seconds each.

Don't Break Brown Agaricus Caps If you break them while they're still brown, you get knocked back and waste time. Hold the Wand of Farming, look at the cap, wait for it to turn red (3-6 seconds), then break it immediately. The red state only lasts a moment.

Mirrorverse

The Mirrorverse is in the West Village area and unlocks with the Supreme Timecharm. It costs 3:30 Rift Time to enter, but time is frozen inside — no rush. It contains 7 puzzle rooms.

  1. Lever Room — Flip levers to match their mirror reflection states.
  2. Path Walking — Your floor is blank but the mirror shows a path of iron blocks over lava. Walk the corresponding path on your side.
  3. Rooms 3-5 — Escalating puzzles. Room 4 has an optional hard mode for a talisman. Room 5 (Red/Green) rewards the Big Brain Talisman.
  4. Dance Room — Stand on blocks and follow instructions. A hole appears leading to Room 7. Keep dancing for the Tiny Dancer accessory.
  5. Tubulator (Vertical Parkour) — Blank room with invisible blocks. Right-click mirror blocks to make them appear on your side. Complete the parkour. SkyHanni/Skytils waypoints make this trivial.

After completing all rooms, talk to Dr. Emmett. Claim 60,000 Motes (one-time), then buy the Mirrorverse Timecharm for 10,000 Motes + 10 Shadow Cruxes. Secure it in the Rift Gallery.

Progress Saves If you run out of Rift Time mid-Mirrorverse, your progress is saved. Talk to Dr. Emmett (circling particles at the entrance) to teleport to any completed room on your next visit.

Berberis Farming Guide

This is the main reason most mid-game players enter the Rift. Berberis farming is accessible as soon as you secure the Supreme Timecharm (Timecharm #1) and can realistically earn 10M+ coins/hr.

How It Works

  1. Go to the Dreadfarm (requires Supreme Timecharm).
  2. Find Wilted Berberis — dead bushes with purple particles. Harvest one and the particles jump to the next bush. Follow the chain.
  3. Craft into Wilted Berberis Bunches (20 Wilted Berberis = 1 bunch) in the Rift crafting table.
  4. Transfer bunches out via the Rift Transfer Chest (unlocked by bringing a Stability Elixir to Inverted Sirius).
  5. Sell on the Bazaar in normal SkyBlock.

Tips for Efficiency

  • Stay in one area — Berberis spawn follows your break order, so sticking to a loop is faster than running across the whole farm.
  • More Rift Time = more profit per visit. Collect Enigma Souls aggressively — each one adds 20 seconds of farming time.
  • Start farming immediately after getting the Supreme Timecharm. Even with low Rift Time, short farming sessions add up. You don't need all 8 Timecharms to start making money.
  • Check Bazaar prices before selling. Berberis bunch prices fluctuate — use sell offers for better rates if you're not in a rush.
Expected Income With just the Supreme Timecharm and a few Enigma Souls: ~5-8M coins/hr. With more Rift Time from additional Timecharms and souls: 10-15M+/hr. Even short sessions are very profitable compared to most other mid-game methods.

🌋 Crimson Isle & Kuudra

The Crimson Isle is the largest and most complex island in SkyBlock, unlocked at Combat Level 22. It contains an entire faction questline, a boss fight system (Kuudra) comparable to Dungeons, the Dojo, lava fishing, and Trophy Fish. This is where mid-game becomes late-game.

Getting Started

  • Reach Combat Level 22 and warp to the Crimson Isle Use /warp crimson. The island is immediately hostile — mobs are much stronger than The End. Bring decent gear.
  • Choose a Faction — Mage or Barbarian Talk to Elle at the entrance. Both factions have their own multi-step storyline. You must complete at least one to unlock Kuudra. Mage faction focuses on magic and Intelligence. Barbarian focuses on melee. You can do both eventually.
  • Build faction reputation Reputation unlocks emissary shops and Kuudra Keys. Earn it by completing faction quests, killing mobs, and doing daily activities. This is a sustained grind, not a one-day task.
  • Complete the Dojo for a free Belt 7 skill-based challenges (Force, Stamina, Mastery, Tenacity, etc.) that strip all your gear. Score determines belt rank — Black Belt gives 12% damage reduction. Purely skill-based, so even undergeared players can earn a belt. Belts go in your Equipment Belt slot.
  • Get the Abiphone Buy from Udel (Mage) or Nuvian (Barbarian) on the Crimson Isle. Lets you remotely call NPCs — Maddox for slayer quests, Ophelia for quiver refills, Jacob for contest info, and 55 more contacts. Each unique contact gives +10 SkyBlock XP. Huge quality-of-life upgrade.

Kuudra

Vanquishers

Time-Limited! Diana is only mayor part of the time — check who the current mayor is with /mayor. If it's Diana, drop everything and do this event.

Sweep & Toughness Deep Dive

Sweep is your axe's ability to cut multiple logs from a tree in a single chop. It builds from gear, Heart of the Forest perks, and your Foraging level. Toughness is how much a tree resists your Sweep — every tree type has its own Toughness value.

Tree TypeToughnessNotes
Park Trees (Oak, Birch, Jungle)3–5Easy to clear with minimal Sweep. Good for starting out.
Fig Tree7Your first Galatea tree. Drops Fig for Figstone gear.
Mangrove Tree50Requires high Sweep to farm efficiently. Best money tree.
Ancient Trees100+Endgame content. Need maxed HotF perks + Figstone gear.

Formula: If your Sweep ≥ tree's Toughness, you cut the whole tree. If Sweep < Toughness, you only cut a portion. Stack Sweep from Heart of the Forest perks, Figstone Armor bonus, and Foraging level milestones.

Heart of the Forest Priority

Unlock perks in this order for the most efficient progression:

  1. Sweep perks first — the most impactful stat. Without enough Sweep, you can't efficiently farm higher-Toughness trees at all.
  2. Fortune perks — increases log drops per tree. Direct money increase once you can already clear trees.
  3. Tree Gift Quality — improves the rarity and value of Tree Gifts. Lower priority because gifts are RNG-based.
  4. XP perks — useful for leveling Foraging faster but doesn't directly affect earnings.

Hunting (New Skill — June 2025)

The Galatea update introduced Hunting as a separate skill from Foraging. Hunting involves tracking and killing creatures that spawn across all SkyBlock islands — not just Galatea. It's a passive progression system you'll engage with naturally while playing other content.

  • Creatures spawn everywhere — every island has huntable creatures. You'll encounter them while farming, mining, doing slayers, etc.
  • Kill creatures → earn Hunting XP + Attribute Shards. Shards can be syphoned into permanent profile-wide stat buffs through the Hunting interface.
  • The Hunting Box — an inventory widget that tracks nearby creatures. Shows creature type, tier, and direction.
  • Creature tiers — range from common to rare. Higher-tier creatures give more XP and better shards but are tougher to kill.
  • Attribute system — collected shards unlock permanent stat boosts (Health, Defense, Strength, etc.) that apply across your entire profile.
Passive Progression Hunting levels independently from Foraging — it's its own skill with its own XP bar. You don't need to grind it separately. Just kill huntable creatures when you see them while doing other content. The attribute shards add up over time and the stat buffs are permanent.

Enchanting

How It Works

Enchanting XP comes almost entirely from the Experimentation Table. Non-table methods exist but are daily capped.

Experimentation Table Strategy

  • Three experiments per day: Chronomatron, Ultrasequencer, and Superpairs. Each earns XP + rewards.
  • Superpairs is where the real loot is — T7 enchant books can sell for millions.
  • After claiming all three, renew up to 3 times daily using Bits + XP levels. Worth doing if you have Booster Cookie access for Bits.
  • Use Skyblocker/SkyHanni solvers for Chronomatron and Ultrasequencer — makes hitting chain 12+ trivial.

Non-Table Method (Daily Capped)

Growth 4 Loop: Buy Leather Helmets from Armor Smith (8 coins each). Splash 1 Grand Experience Bottle, enchant Growth 4. Splash another, remove Growth 4. Repeat. ~1.6k Enchanting XP per cycle. Not amazing, but supplements daily table XP.

Progression Path

  2. Craft Experimentation Table — do ALL daily experiments from now on, never skip a day
  3. Renew experiments with Bits for extra runs
  4. Use Growth 4 loop to supplement daily XP
  5. Get a Guardian pet and level it through experiments
  6. Push for Enchanting 60 over time — this is a slow skill, patience required

  • Enchanted Books — Always check if an item has enchants before selling it. Books on gear often contain valuable enchants that should be transferred first.
  • Backpack Slots — Tia the Fairy grants backpack slots as you turn in Fairy Souls. You still need to craft or buy actual backpacks (Small, Medium, Large, Greater) to fill those slots.
